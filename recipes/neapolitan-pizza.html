Neapolitan Pizza

Source: eightydishes

The Pizza

Neapolitan pizza originated in Naples, Italy, hundreds of years ago. Why mess with a good thing? There is even an Italian non-profit organization—the True Neapolitan Pizza Association (Associazione Verace Pizza Napoletana), or AVPN—which is dedicated entirely to protecting the integrity and tradition that go into an authentic Neapolitan pizza.

According to the AVPN, traditional Neapolitan pizza has a few qualities that make it immediately recognizable:

  • Round shape: Sorry, square pizza evangelists. Round is the way for this particular style.
  • Sleek crust: This pizza has a raised edge of about two centimeters and should never be burned. The crust is often soft with a pleasant elasticity.
  • Tomatoes: If canned, they should be crushed by hand. If fresh, they should be sliced.
  • Cheese: Mozzarella slices should be spread evenly across the pizza.
  • Garnishes: Fresh basil and extra virgin olive oil top the whole thing off.

Ingredients

The Dough
  • ¾ cup lukewarm water
  • 1 teaspoon active-dry yeast
  • 10 ounces unbleached all-purpose flour
  • 1.5 teaspoons kosher salt
The Garnishes
  • San Marzano Tomatoes
  • Olive Oil
  • Oregano
  • Basil

Assembly

  1. Make the dough: Mix flour, water, salt, and yeast together until the desired consistency is achieved. Then roll out into a disk.
  2. Make the sauce: Crush or slice your tomatoes, simmer with olive oil, and season appropriately while remembering that the beauty of this dish is simplicity.
  3. Assemble the pizza: Spread your sauce and space mozzarella slices evenly around the surface area.
  4. Bake the pizza: Tradition dictates that the cooking should be done in a high-heat oven for a very short period of time. It should also be cooked exclusively in a wood-fired oven, but I won’t tell if you won’t.
  5. Finish with garnishes: Add a drizzle of olive oil, a sprinkle of oregano, and a few fresh basil leaves. Enjoy!
